As a result , most viewers will go into " Jobs , " a biopic about the Apple co founder and black turtlenecked guru of the technocratic age , knowing two fundamental things about him : that he was a visionary perfectionist obsessed with melding technological form and function into products that would tickle the hearts and minds of the people who used them and that he was so possessed by that mission that he could be a control freak dictator , a fanatic , an over the top jerk . Still , watching Jobs , with its basic warts and all accuracy and shrewd , unsweetened performance by Ashton Kutcher ( who was obviously cast because he looks like Jobs , but who bites into the role with his incisors ) , I was surprised and frequently compelled by what a starkly honest portrait it is .
