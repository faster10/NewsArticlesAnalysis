Outsized health care spending would make sense if the U . S . got markedly better care than the rest of the world . But that isn t the case . The U . S . does have some of the world s best hospitals and advanced care breast cancer survival rates in the U . S . are the highest in the world . But the data on this are not uniform for example , childhood leukemia survival rates here are not as good as those in Canada or Germany . And on overall measures of health care , the U . S . is below average : It s 27th out of 34 developed countries in life expectancy , and 28th in the number of doctors per capita .
