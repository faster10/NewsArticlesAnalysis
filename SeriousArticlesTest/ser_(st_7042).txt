Easier credit conditions and lower interest rates are supposed to boost growth by stimulating investment and consumption demand . But in the core of the euro zone countries like Germany and the Netherlands credit has been plentiful , and interest rates have been close to zero for some time , so there was never much chance that bond purchases would have a significant impact there . And , indeed , the European Commission ' s most recent economic forecast shows that spending in the core countries has not increased as a result of the ECB ' s policies Germany ' s external surplus is actually increasing .
