The next eight years brought a rollercoaster of results , and a revolving door for the captains . Hutchins s successor , bizarrely , was not even British . Warren Jacques hailed from Sydney , made his name by coaching the 1985 Wimbledon finalist Kevin Curren , and was then hired by the Lawn Tennis Association in a similar leap of faith to the one that brought Bob Brett in last year . The concept of an overseas Davis Cup captain was so unfamiliar that special dispensation had to be sought from the International Tennis Federation . As Jacques gave way to Billy Knight , and then to Tony Pickard , results continued to slide .
