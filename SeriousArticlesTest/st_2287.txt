There was significantly more to him than the natural warmth and wit from which he fashioned his gloriously successful career ? that was the equivalent of the iceberg s tip . You don t occupy a berth on the top floor of Hotel Showbiz for 50 years by accident or luck . Wogan was our other monarch . He was the king of British light entertainment , a twinkling wizard of small screen whimsy and a river running through our radios . Perhaps only Sir David Attenborough matches him for career longevity , sustained success and national affection . Just think : what would the BBC be without the pair of them ?
