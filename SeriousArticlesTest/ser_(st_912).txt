Campaigns and their supporters have long sought to strike a difficult balance between building robust political operations and conserving cash for use in moments of maximum impact . POLITICO s analysis assesses that balance by using Federal Election Commission records detailing fundraising and spending through the end of last year ? which were released on Sunday night ? plus advertising data provided by The Tracking Firm showing television and radio ad spending through January . The analysis does not take into account January tallies for funds raised or expenses accrued on things other than radio and TV buys , though such advertising is typically the most expensive cost for campaigns and super PACs .
