In the world of scientific peer review , there s a new crowd in town : 11 year olds . Over the past four years , thousands of fifth and sixth graders from more than 30 U . S . states and 18 other countries have been judges in an annual competition called the Flame Challenge . The contest , the brainchild of actor Alan Alda , rates grown up scientists on their answers to basic questions about how the world works . The point isn t to judge the accuracy of the responses but to see how well the scientists can use their explanations to engage and inspire the students .
