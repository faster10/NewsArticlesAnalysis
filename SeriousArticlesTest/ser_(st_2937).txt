At one point Kate sits in her attic , surrounded by tangible reminders of her husband ' s former life , and as she clicks through a series of revealing slide photographs Haigh ' s camera stays on Rampling ' s face . It ' s an endlessly photogenic camera object , to state the obvious . But what Rampling accomplishes in this scene , and what we see Kate go through in the final three or four minutes ( again , wordlessly ) of " 45 Years , " leaves us with a full yet destabilizing sense of wonder . Coming off his previous excellent film , " Week end , " Haigh reasserts his masterly touch with the mysteries , vagaries and possibilities of the human heart .
