Priorities in tennis are horribly warped . The instinct among the suits , when faced with the spectre of match fixing , is to leap on the defensive about all their noble work . But in a quieter moment , they might usefully reflect on the imbalances of a sport where the winner of the men s final in Melbourne collects over ? 1 . 8 million and a first round loser in futures receives barely enough for a ready meal . And they might recognise at last that theirs is a world conditioned to attract the corruption that they so condemn .
