In the euro zone ' s case , the risk is compounded by the ineffectiveness of its key macroprudential instrument , the Stability and Growth Pact , in limiting countries ' spending , as lower interest rates give debtor countries leeway to spend more . Public debt as a share of GDP is on the rise in Italy and Spain , even though both countries , with their euro zone partners , have committed to reducing the debt ratio . In fact , even without lower interest rates , the deficit limits that the pact imposes have proved to be non binding , exemplified by France ' s flouting of them since 2009 .
