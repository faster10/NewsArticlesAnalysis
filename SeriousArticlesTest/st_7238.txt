The Pilgrims appear , for the most part , to have recognized that they would be establishing the Plymouth Colony at the sufferance of the local Wampanoag Indian population , or not at all . They made at least a token effort to pay for the land they occupied , or at least to ask permission . This was a departure from the typical colonial practices of the time , and it showed a pragmatic realism that differentiated these Separatists from , say , the opportunists who were attempting to establish Jamestown in Virginia as a moneymaking land grab .
