Putin repeated his proposition for a global coalition against Islamic State that he first made at the United Nations in September . He railed against Turkey for shooting down a Russian warplane near the Syrian border last week , warning that the Kremlin s response to the heinous war crime wouldn t be limited to an embargo on Turkish tomatoes or construction companies . Little in the president s speech was new or surprising . What was telling were the things he chose not to say . If his last address to the nation was all about Russia s glorious annexation of Crimea and its never ending struggle against American hegemony , this year Putin didn t mention Ukraine a single time .
