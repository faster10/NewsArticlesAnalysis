Oil futures extended last Friday s 10 per cent rally by more than 1 per cent in early Asian trade on Monday on short covering demand , with both the benchmark Brent and US crude grades hitting highs of US 32 . 69 ( S 47 ) and US 32 . 64 a barrel , respectively . However , they fell back into negative territory after Iraq s Oil Ministry told Reuters yesterday that crude output had reached a record high last month , with its fields producing as much as 4 . 13 million barrels a day . Brent fell back to US 31 . 34 in early afternoon trade in London while US crude dipped to US 31 . 24 .
