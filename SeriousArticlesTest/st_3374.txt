Still , even rough numbers might help open a discussion of whether we get the health care we pay for . Sanders would pay for his proposal with a combination of employer contributions , payroll taxes , and an increase in top tax rates . The conventional wisdom is that anything that includes the words taxes is enough of a turn off to make Americans run in fear . Health care economist Gerard Anderson of Johns Hopkins says that even if single payer is more effective and costs less , a trillion dollar tax increase is not something the American public is willing to support .
