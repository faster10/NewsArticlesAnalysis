The push to raise the rim began in the mid to late 1980s after statistics showed the average height of NBA players reached a peak of 6 7 . 62 . Team owners felt that the challenge for taller than average players had lessened , while salaries for top performers continued to climb . For years , agents for average and underheight players fought against the change , arguing that the additional 1 increase would present shorter athletes with a disadvantage , and fundamentally alter the nature of the game .
