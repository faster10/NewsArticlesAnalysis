Pharmaceutical conglomerates that are major contributors to the GOP also claimed higher profits . During the recession of 2002 , the largest ten drug companies in the Fortune 500 reported combined profits of 35 . 9 billion more than the profits of all of the other 490 businesses combined . In 2003 , Bush signed a prescription drug benefit for Medicare , which will give these major contributors to Bush ' s election a record 139 billion in profits over eight years when it begins in 2006 . In addition , a new tax break will allow American drug conglomerates to return foreign profits of 75 billion to the U . S . at a 5 . 25 percent tax rate , compared to the standard 35 percent rate . No wonder pharmaceuticals predict increased profits .
