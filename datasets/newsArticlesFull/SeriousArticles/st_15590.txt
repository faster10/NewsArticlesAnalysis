For President Obama , the effort to sell the potentially legacy making agreement may be the last big battle of his tenure . He will have to rely mostly on Republican votes , while holding on to support from the few Democratic allies willing to confront organized labour and other liberal groups that are skeptical about trade globalization . " We have a whole of government , whole of White House effort underway , " said Obama ' s trade representative Michael B Froman who has negotiated the agreement since 2009 .
