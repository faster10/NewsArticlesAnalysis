The White House and congressional leaders may have reached a tentative budget deal Monday night , but conservatives are making it known that they ' re not thrilled about the compromise . Almost as soon as GOP lawmakers emerged from a briefing with party leaders Monday , they started expressing their doubts over the 80 billion agreement that would increase defense and domestic spending over the next two years and raise the debt limit . In addition to voicing concerns about not reducing spending , GOP lawmakers also saw the deal as a concession to Democrats . " Based on what I know now , it appears the president got whatever he wanted , " said Alabama Sen . Jeff Sessions .
