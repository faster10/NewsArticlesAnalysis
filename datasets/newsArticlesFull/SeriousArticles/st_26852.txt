Obama also ignored his generals in 2011 when it came to implementing his 2008 campaign promise to withdraw U . S . soldiers from Iraq . The administration failed to reach a deal to extend the presence of U . S . troops in the country in 2011 , in part because Obama demanded that Iraq s parliament vote to approve it , which was extremely unlikely . Gen . Martin Dempsey , the chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff at the time , told Congress that no one in the chain of command for the military favored a complete withdrawal from Iraq . He would later say the job of securing a stable Iraq was left unfinished because of the premature exit .
