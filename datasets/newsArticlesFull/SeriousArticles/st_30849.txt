He was first asked to commentate the 1970 Eurovision contest in Dublin for BBC Radio and saw , from the offset , that the whole thing was waiting to be sent up . European high culture is tediously homogenous , with the same kind of classical music , opera , food and wine being served up in every capital city every night . It s the low culture that sets nations apart . Eurovision asks each country to pick a song it thinks the rest of Europe will vote for . Some do so with spectacular success but , for most , it s an evening of abject failure . As Wogan realised , this is rich in comic potential .
