In the light of a controversial nuclear deal with Iran that has seen sanctions on the country lifted , the covering of statues has been taken as an international scandal . Luca Squeri , an Italian member of parliament , suggested that it was not an act of respect from Rome but one of " submission . " Nervana Mahmoud , a writer who frequently blogs about the Middle East , called it a " shambolic appeasement of Islamism . " Some unfavorably contrasted the Italian attitude to that of the French , who had boldly and stereotypically refused to not serve wine in meetings with the Iranians .
