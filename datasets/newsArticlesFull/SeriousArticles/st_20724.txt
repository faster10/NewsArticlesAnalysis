Inevitably , the alphabet soup of EU acronyms and jargon will encourage cynicism , not least among the many Tory backbenchers who regard the whole exercise as preposterous . Is the proposed amendment to regulation no 492 2011 on freedom of movement for workers within the union really an emergency brake on EU migrants claiming in work benefits ? Would the suggested red flag make much difference to the ability of member state parliaments to thwart EU legislation ? Is Tusk s letter more than chicken feed ? On the final question , those close to the negotiation say : why do you think it has been so tense ? Why have these talks absorbed so much of the prime minister and George Osborne s time ? Precisely because they were substantial and significant .
