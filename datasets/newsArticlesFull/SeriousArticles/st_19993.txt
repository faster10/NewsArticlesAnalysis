It is very unlikely that Zika virus established local transmission in New South Wales as the mosquitoes , Aedes aegypti , that spread the infection are not established here ? although they are found in some parts of North Queensland , Dr Vicky Sheppeard , the state s communicable diseases director , said in a statement after confirming Australia s first cases of the virus this year . Since 2014 , occasional cases of Zika have been identified in New South Wales among people who travelled to areas where the virus has been most commonly transmitted , the statement said .
