In recent weeks , Mr Cameron has nevertheless played his hand more skilfully than some can bring themselves to admit . His diplomacy has produced rewards which , in Plato s Republic rather than Daily Mail dominated Britain , might actually be seen as quite rational and effective . He has also , thus far , managed to minimise the scale of revolt , as Theresa May s support underscores . The prime minister is outmanoeuvring his backbench critics too . Increasingly , he is also making a good case for Britain in Europe , which he should have made long ago . In this argument , he is on the right side . But it is high time for the case for Britain in Europe to be made more strongly by all those who believe in it , and who can reach the parts of the electorate that Mr Cameron never can .
