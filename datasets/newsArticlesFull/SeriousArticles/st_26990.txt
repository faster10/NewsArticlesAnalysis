Road funding : Fed or banks ? Congressional negotiators are facing a choice over how to pay for part of a new highway spending bill : by tapping a rainy day fund at the Federal Reserve or cutting federal payments to some of the country s biggest banks . The New York Times writes about separate Senate and House passed bills that lawmakers will mold into one measure . The Senate plan would reduce the Fed s annual dividend payments to large commercial banks , while the House s version preserves dividends and instead requires the Fed to provide 59 . 5 billion over 10 years instead of putting the money into an account intended to cover potential losses .
