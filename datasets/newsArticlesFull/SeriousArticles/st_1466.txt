Martinez was the preeminent designated hitter of his age and while the position allowed him to excel on one side of the ball he did so at a level that still makes him worthy . Heck , the award that goes to the most outstanding designated hitter is named for him . At his peak , Martinez hit . 329 with a . 574 slugging percentage and averaged 28 homers over a seven year period . He wasn t able to contribute anything positive defensively to his WAR score and still he ranked with some of the best two way players of the era , like Barry Bonds and names you ll see on my ballot ( Bagwell , Griffey ) .
