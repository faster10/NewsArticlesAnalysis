At present , the schemes that have the potential to unlock over 20 , 000 tonnes of unused gold valued at over Rs 55 lakh crore , face a dearth of collection centres . Jewellers across the country are enthused with the proposition of turning themselves into collection and hallmarking centres for the government . They said it will not only ease supply crunch but will help bring more gold into the system . Modi said once the goldsmiths of India gain familiarity with the schemes , they could become the biggest agents of these schemes .
