Anders Ericsson studied elite musicians at the Berlin Academy of Music and is credited with the now wildly popular notion that it takes 10 , 000 hours of deliberate practice to master anything . But the key that separated the truly great from the good and the adequate was the way they spent those 10 , 000 hours : they practiced intensely , first thing in the morning when they were freshest , for no more than 60 to 90 minutes at a time , and they rested more . In other words , like brain waves , like heart beats , they worked in short bursts and recovered in leisure and became excellent at what they did .
