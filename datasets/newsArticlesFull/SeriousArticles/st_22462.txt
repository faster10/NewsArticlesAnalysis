It goes on to define serious provocation as ? conduct sufficient to excite an intense passion in a reasonable person " and to note that the burden of proof is on the defendant to prove either mitigating factor by a preponderance of the evidence . The burden of proof , however , remains on the State to prove beyond a reasonable doubt each of the elements of first degree murder and , when appropriately raised , the absence of circumstances at the time of the killing that would justify or exonerate the killing under the principles stated in Article 7 of this Code .
