In spite a global supply glut , most traders expect Opec at its Friday meeting in Vienna to endorse its decision from last year to pump oil vigorously to protect its market share from non members like United States and Russia . Only a few Opec members , such as Venezuela and Iran , are hoping for output cuts to stabilise crude prices which have tumbled from highs above 100 a barrel in June 2014 . " The market is vulnerable to short covering spikes if anything unexpected on Opec comes out , " said Peter Donovan , broker at Liquidity Energy in New York .
