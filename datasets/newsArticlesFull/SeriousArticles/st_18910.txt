One of the four protesters remaining at the Malheur National Wildlife Refuge said in a darkly lit video posted on Friday that he wanted to be assured he would not be arrested if he left . Others with him expressed similar sentiments . Tensions in the standoff remained high four days after Robert " LaVoy " Finicum , 54 , a spokesman for the group that seized buildings at the refuge on January 2 , was killed by police during the arrests of occupation leader Ammon Bundy and several other protesters as they traveled on a highway .
