Convincing the Labour party , as well as some Conservative MPs and the sceptical Liberal Democrats and SNP , of the truth and accuracy of this latter claim about a wider strategy will be essential if a Commons majority is to be achieved . They say , rightly , that a few British bombs , however skilfully aimed , will not change the course of the battle against Isis . Such risky operations may only be worthwhile if accompanied by a broader effort by an enlarged military coalition , including Russia , Turkey and leading Arab states , supported ( or at least not sabotaged ) by Iran , and conducted in tandem with a genuine diplomatic push for peace . The government should admit this is not something it can be sure of delivering .
