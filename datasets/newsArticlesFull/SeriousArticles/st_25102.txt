The central controversy in Bergdahl s story centers on his decision to leave his post . Bergdahl says that he left in order to travel by foot from his platoon s small outpost , O . P . Mest , to a large Forward Operating Base , F . O . B . Sharana , to alert Army superiors about dangerous leadership problems in his unit . The journey was long and treacherous , through Taliban country , but Bergdahl was an experienced outdoorsman and a confident soldier , an idealist who thought he could make it . Bergdahl s goal , he says , was to cause a DUSTWUN : a radio alarm signal meaning Duty Status Whereabouts Unknown ( the Army s version of man overboard , Koenig tells us ) , which would generate attention and get his message heard .
