The NLD gathered millions of signatures in support of changing these clauses , but all attempts to amend the constitution over the last two years have failed ? and this has probably been the most significant setback for Myanmar s reforms . Indeed , having awakened so many hopes when it began in 2011 , the entire reform process now appears stalled . In sum , the deeply unpopular military , faced with likely electoral humiliation at the hands of the NLD and the dozens of other ethnic minority opposition parties at this election , has stubbornly clung on to all the constitutional safeguards that will protect its power . As a result , even though the NLD and other opposition parties should win the most seats on Sunday , their subsequent power to change the country for the better will be severely constrained .
