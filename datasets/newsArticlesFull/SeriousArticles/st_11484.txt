CBS News correspondent Elizabeth Palmer reports the final push to retake Ramadi came Monday after weeks of preparation , as Iraqi troops surrounded the town and cut off ISIS supply lines . Gen . Ahmed al Belawi said that the prime minister kicked off the visit by meeting security and provincial officials for the latest updates . This comes as earlier military spokesman announced that Ramadi , the capital of Anbar province , had been " fully liberated " . The spokesperson added that ISIS fighters still controlled 30 percent of Ramadi and the forces still do not control many districts from which the ISIS fighters have retreated at the current moment .
