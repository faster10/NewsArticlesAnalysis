Belgium s Interior Minister Jan Jambon , left , walks by a police officer as he arrives to take part in a National Security Council meeting in Brussels on Sunday , Nov . 22 , 2015 . Western leaders stepped up the rhetoric against the Islamic State group on Sunday as residents of the Belgian capital awoke to largely empty streets and the city entered its second day under the highest threat level . With a menace of Paris style attacks against Brussels and a missing suspect in the deadly Nov . 13 attacks in France last spotted crossing into Belgium , the city kept subways and underground trams closed for a second day .
