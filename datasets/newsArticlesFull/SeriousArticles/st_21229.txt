If the NFL had blown through all its bylaws just to settle the Los Angeles question , get its new stadium in a big market and increase revenue , that would at least be understandable , if not exactly ethically palatable . But to take those liberties with rule abiding fans and officials in St . Louis and use them just to keep taxpayers in San Diego and fans in Oakland adrift ? In a league that has spent an average of 250 million in public funds on stadiums during the past 20 years and is about to suck up about 750 million more between stadium projects in Minneapolis and Atlanta , the rules don t matter as much as the results .
