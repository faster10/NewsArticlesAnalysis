Mr Osborne has his sights on an ambitious ? and courageous ? reordering of the British state . Yet the apparently immutable protection given to the elderly by securing the value of their pensions , together with the priority given to expenditure on the NHS , means that the UK will increasingly become a state that hands out help to the old and sick rather than investing in the young . Lower spending on education and investment will quite possibly affect future growth and productivity . If Mr Osborne is to pursue his end of a slimmer state , some of those ringfences need dismantling .
