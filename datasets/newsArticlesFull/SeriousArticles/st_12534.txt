Refugees as shutdown threat : The Hill writes that the fight over blocking refugees from Syria and Iraq has emerged as one of the biggest hurdles to Congress finishing work on a yearlong spending bill and preventing a government shutdown next month . Lawmakers will return from their Thanksgiving break with just two weeks to reach a deal before the Dec . 11 deadline . Conservatives see the funding bill as their best vehicle to force the White House to bend on the refugee issue , the Hill says . They were emboldened by a victory last week , in which 47 Democrats defied a White House veto threat and backed a Republican bill to boost screening requirements for refugees .
