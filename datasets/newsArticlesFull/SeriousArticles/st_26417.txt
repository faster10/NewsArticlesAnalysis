In many states , like Florida , if a person is convicted of a crime against children , it automatically triggers registration to the sex offender registry . Thirty eight states include juvenile sex offenders in their sex offender registries . Alaska , Florida and Maine will register juveniles only if they are tried as adults . Indiana registers juveniles age 14 and older . South Dakota registers juveniles age 15 and older . Most states allow public access to sex offender registries via the Internet and anyone with a computer can locate registered sex offenders in their neighborhoods .
