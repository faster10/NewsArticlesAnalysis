If anything good has come out of the Paris massacre , it is the growing solidarity among the opponents of this fanaticism . Hitherto , the Western powers have been divided over what to do about Syria . America has insisted that the brutal dictator Bashar al Assad must go Russia wants him to stay in place . Bombing campaigns have pursued contrary objectives . Britain , curiously , has carried out air strikes in Iraq but not in Syria . In these pages , Liam Fox , the former defence secretary , and Lord Dannatt , previously chief of the general staff , make the case for putting boots on the ground .
