The unprecedented rainfall some areas received two thirds of the annual rainfall within a few hours has triggered a debate among meteorologists whether it was a fallout of climate change and global warming . Experts however are divided on the issue . While some argue that it is a normal seasonal phenomenon others link it to El Nino effect triggered by global warming and climate change . YK Reddy points out that sustained and long term research is needed to link the recent excess rains to climate change .
