The party wants the government s proposal to pass five tests before it will agree to back the bombing . These include : legality , which it believes is met by the UN resolution creation of a post Islamic State plan work towards a no bombing zone for civilians pressure on Gulf states to get more involved and carrying out a domestic investigation into jihadi funding . Tim Farron , the Lib Dem leader , warned against a kneejerk reaction as he spoke in the House of Commons , suggesting the whole party will vote against .
