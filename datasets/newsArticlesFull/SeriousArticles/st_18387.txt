MANUFACTURING KEEPS SLOWING ? FT s Shawn Donnan : US manufacturers last month recorded their largest contraction in demand in more than six years as the strong dollar and low oil prices continued to drag on the economy . The Institute for Supply Management s monthly manufacturing index slipped to 48 . 2 in December , marking the second month in a row that it had fallen below 50 , the line between contraction and expansion . The reading was the weakest since June 2009 and marked the first time since the immediate aftermath of the global financial crisis that the US manufacturing sector has seen consecutive contractions .
