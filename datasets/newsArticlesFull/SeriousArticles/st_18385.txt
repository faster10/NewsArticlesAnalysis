WALL STREET S LATEST CONFLICT ? NYT s Andrew Ross Sorkin : It goes by a rather innocuous sounding name , the sort of phrase you might breeze past in a loan document : designated lender counsel . But pay attention , because it s the latest conflict ridden practice on Wall Street . Over the last several years , a new , insidious relationship has quietly developed between the nation s largest private equity firms , the banks that lend them billions to fund their buyouts and the law firms that advise on these deals . Historically , when a bank , like JPMorgan Chase , made a loan to a private equity firm planning a big acquisition , like the Blackstone Group , the bank would hire an outside law firm to scrutinize the loan and the transaction . But neither the Federal Reserve nor any other regulator has addressed this latest private equity maneuver .
