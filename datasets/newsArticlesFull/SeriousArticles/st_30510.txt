Irvin was born Feb . 25 , 1919 , in Haleburg , Ala . , but grew up in Orange , N . J . , and excelled in four sports in high school . He was offered a football scholarship to the University of Michigan , but couldn t afford the move to the Midwest . Instead , Irvin attended Lincoln University in Chester County , Pa . He began his professional baseball career with the Newark ( N . J . ) Eagles of the Negro National League in 1938 , and became one of the team ' s most feared sluggers . The righthanded Irvin hit . 400 in 1940 , and he won the batting title in 1946 with a . 375 average , the same year the Eagles won the Negro League World Series . Irvin spent one season in the Mexican League ( 1942 ) .
