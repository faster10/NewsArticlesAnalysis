MUMBAI : A stable Chinese market and a surprise decision by Bank of Japan ( BoJ ) to move to a negative interest rate regime led to a sharp rebound in the sensex on Friday , which closed 1 . 6 ( 401 points ) higher at 24 , 871 . The day ' s gains , highest single session rise in the new year , were backed by a Rs 570crore net buying by foreign funds . This was a reversal as foreign funds have net sold stocks worth about 1 . 6 billion ( about Rs 11 , 126 crore ) in January , official data showed .
