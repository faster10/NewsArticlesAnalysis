? Your editorial ( 3 February ) argues for restoring the lost link between the EU institutions and the people . You are surely correct . But how is this restoration to begin unless the media , in their many forms , begin to report regularly and extensively on the workings of the European institutions , including commission , council and parliament . One of the dispiriting aspects of the present renegotiation pantomime is the dearth of public knowledge about the European project , the vacuum too often filled by misinformation interspersed with crisis headlines . The EU is far from perfect , but it is a unique achievement deserving far more media attention and scrutiny than it currently gets .
