The Gold Monetisation Scheme ( GMS ) , 2015 will offer option to resident Indians to deposit their precious metal and earn an interest of up to 2 . 5 per cent while under the Sovereign Gold Bonds Scheme , investors can earn an interest rate of 2 . 75 per cent per annum by buying paper bonds . Modi also unveiled the first ever Indian gold coin and bullion , bearing national emblem Ashok Chakra on one side and Mahatma Gandhis image engraved on the other side . Initially , the coins will be available in denominations of 5 and 10 grams . A 20 gram bullion will also be available through 125 MMTC outlets .
