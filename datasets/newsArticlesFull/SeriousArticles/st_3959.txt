US crude ' s West Texas Intermediate futures ( WTI ) were off 1 . 45 , or 3 . 5 percent , at 40 . 40 . Both benchmarks had raced into positive territory earlier on Wednesday , reacting to a headline from Tehran ' s oil ministry news agency Shana that a majority of Opec members agree on output cuts . But prices fell back rapidly as the report also said Saudi Arabia , the kingpin in the Organisation of the Petroleum Exporting Countries , and other Persian Gulf Arab member countries of Opec were not agreeable to the reductions .
