The row about the Oscars looked like the perfect spectator spat : It was about something real . Why was there not a single actor of colour represented in the nominations ? Was there a problem of under representation at the judging level ? What did it say to society that such a toweringly high profile , public facing institution could fail in such an obvious way ? What might it say if , through simple and consistent argumentation , the situation could be resolved ? Add in the global reach , the beauty quotient , and a couple of villains ? Michael Caine telling black actors to be patient Charlotte Rampling worrying that the hashtag OscarsSoWhite was racist to whites ? and you have a storm that is structurally perfect .
