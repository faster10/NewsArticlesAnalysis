Coping with catastrophe is hard enough : intelligence gathering adds to the problem . Consider the research , detailed by this newspaper , that shows that more than 140 Islamists jailed for terror offences are believed to have been released from prison and are free to walk the streets . Keeping tabs on such people , some of whom may remain committed to their warped ideology , is not easy . Of course , the Government would like to add to its protective wall access to the public s phone and internet data stored by private communications companies . The battle against the extremists has profound implications for civil liberties .
