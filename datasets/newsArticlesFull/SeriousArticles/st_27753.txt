There was nothing new or extraordinary in the flawed trial that led to Sheikh Nimr ' s execution on Saturday , and if some of the trials for the 46 others adequately protected defendants ' rights , they would be the anomaly . We have documented longstanding due process violations in Saudi Arabia ' s criminal justice system , which has no penal code , and allows judges to convict people on broadly framed charges , some of which do not resemble recognizable crimes , to quickly dismiss allegations of torture of the accused without investigation and to admit confessions that defendants claim are coerced without investigating those claims .
