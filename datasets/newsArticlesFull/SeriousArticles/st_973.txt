CARIG : The odds of a reunion are highly unlikely , mostly because the Mets have little appetite for the kind of long term deal that Cespedes would command . Rival execs believe he could fetch around 150 million over six years on the open market . Though his meteoric rise after the trade deadline quickly sent his stock soaring , his struggles in the postseason also highlighted reasons for pause . Some of the downturn was hastened by various injuries . The Mets believe he ' s better suited in a corner outfield spot , but Michael Conforto and Curtis Granderson have leftfield and rightfield covered . And despite Cespedes raw power , he doesn ' t fit the team ' s disciplined approach , one that values reaching base .
