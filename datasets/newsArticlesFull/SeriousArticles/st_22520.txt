The incident spawned research on the so called bystander effect : a set of brilliant experiments conducted by John Darley and Bibb Latan , who showed in their 1968 study that as the number of bystanders goes up , the probability of any individual bystander intervening goes down . This is so because each bystander is trying to solve a so called " volunteer ' s dilemma . " If everyone responds , the cost could be high and the volunteers might get in each others ' way . It would be best if only one bystander responded , the thinking goes . If this is so , it is reasonable to ask who it will be and why it should be me .
