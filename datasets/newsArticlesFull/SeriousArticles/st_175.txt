One policy issue that could take center stage in a new bill has to do with the mechanism lawmakers set up in WRRDA 2014 requiring the corps to collect project ideas from locals and forward them to Congress in a report . The provision was key to allowing lawmakers to advance individual projects without violating the earmark ban . But the corps first report , last year , only included a fraction of the proposed projects and left lawmakers flaming mad . The second iteration is due next month , and if lawmakers are again unsatisfied they may move for a legislative fix .
