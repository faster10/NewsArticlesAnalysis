Giving the villains some kind of momentarily convincing provenance is one of the writers ' greatest challenges . Fleming started writing his spy thrillers as the Cold War was gathering steam in the ' 50s the Russians were the obvious enemy , given a baroque twist with his invention of the super secret SMERSH wing of their secret service , the name of which supposedly meant " Death to Spies " . Blofeld ' s organisation SPECTRE employed former Soviet agents who had gone rogue , whether out of greed or sheer rampant psychopathy . As real life diplomacy was increasingly guided by a doctrine of co existence and a spirit of detente , SPECTRE ' s allegiances were cut free and its diabolical activities the target of both MI6 and the KGB , sometimes together .
