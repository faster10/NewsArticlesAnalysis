President Barack Obama clearly intended his Sunday night address to pull the nation together behind his strategy for fighting Islamic State . But it appears to have widened divides over strategy . Mr Obama is prescribing more of a slow but steady approach to dealing with ISIS . His critics are advocating more of a fast and furious attempt to knock out ISIS and , by extension , its terror threat . Any quest to bridge these divides is complicated by the fact that the ISIS fight now has become entangled with even more divisive topics : immigration , refugee policy and gun control .
