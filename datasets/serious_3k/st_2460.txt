But the framework for the negotiations already looks obsolete . Hammered out in 2012 by Kofi Annan , then the U . N . peace envoy to Syria , its terms calling for a transitional governing body by mutual consent of all parties , a national dialogue , free elections , and a comprehensive review of the constitution hark back to a time when al Assad seemed weak , the opposition was unified , and the phrase " Arab Spring " could be spoken hopefully in the West . The major powers that helped forge the Geneva Communiqu , perhaps anticipating al Assad ' s fall , refused to place their weight behind it when it mattered . Annan quit his job in frustration .
