Will the downing of the Russian fighter jet by the Turkish armed forces over Turkey s war torn border with Syria on November 24 go down in history as the Franz Ferdinand moment of our century ? Could it trigger a chain of events culminating in the next big confrontation between great powers ? The short answer is no : despite the severity of the crisis ( this is the first time a NATO member has shot down a Russian war plane in over fifty years ) , none of the actors involved have an interest in escalating military tensions .
