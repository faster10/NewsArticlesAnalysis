The underlying causes of the expanding jihadi phenomenon have been well rehearsed , but western countries , Britain included , appear no closer to finding effective answers . The civil war or fitna within Islam between hardline Sunni and Shia Muslims , the chronic poverty and underdevelopment experienced by most Muslim countries , and the overall failure of the Arab spring to dislodge corrupt , authoritarian and undemocratic regimes , such as Sisi s in Egypt , are all powerful triggers for revolt . Then there is the long , damaging ? and unfinished ? history of western interventionism in the Middle East .
