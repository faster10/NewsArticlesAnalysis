Seven months of air strikes in Yemen by Saudi Arabia and other US allied Gulf Arab countries have yet to loosen the Houthis ' grip on the capital Sanaa and make headway toward restoring the now Saudi based Yemeni government to power . The United States and Britain are supporting the coalition with intelligence and both are long time arms suppliers to their Gulf Arab allies . Human rights groups have voiced concern at the mounting death toll from aerial bombing and ground fighting raging across Yemen . Amnesty International has recommended an arms embargo on coalition states , citing repeated bombing of Yemeni civilians .
