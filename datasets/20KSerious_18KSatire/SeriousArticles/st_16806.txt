Of course , in acknowledging his moral complicity , Gr ning fell short of confessing legal guilt . Indeed , for years he has insisted in his innocence as a matter of law , arguing , in an interview with Der Spiegel in 2005 , that he had been no more than a " cog in the gears . " At Auschwitz , Gr ning was responsible for inspecting the luggage of deportees to the camp most of whom were gassed within hours of their arrival for banknotes that would be carefully counted and sent to Berlin . In performing this task , Gr ning insists that he never engaged in cruel , murderous or sadistic acts a claim that we can probably accept as true .
