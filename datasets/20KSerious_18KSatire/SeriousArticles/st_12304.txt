While the United States , which led the negotiations , has tried to promote the deal as the beginning of a new chapter in relations with the Islamic Republic , the agreement has been greeted with suspicion and trepidation across much of the Middle East . For Israel and Sunni states such as Saudi Arabia , Shiite Iran is seen as a destabilizing force . They fear Tehran will now redouble what they see as efforts to dominate the region by supporting anti Israel militant groups , Syrian President Bashar Assad and Shiite Houthi rebels battling a Saudi led coalition in Yemen .
