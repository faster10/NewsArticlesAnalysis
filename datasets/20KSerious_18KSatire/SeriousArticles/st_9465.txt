But few places hold memories more special to me than my hometown . Many people forget that the Great Migration of the last century made Gary , Ind . , birthplace of Michael Jackson , a thriving destination for blacks escaping the Jim Crow South . Lost in the modern narrative of a steel city on hard times is Gary s legacy as an inspirational center of black political power . That power reached its apex with the 1972 National Black Political Convention , where Richard G . Hatcher , one of the first black mayors of a major city , hosted 8 , 000 delegates and a who s who of activists , from Amiri Baraka to Jesse Jackson , to set a national agenda for African Americans to address poverty , jobs and disenfranchisement .
