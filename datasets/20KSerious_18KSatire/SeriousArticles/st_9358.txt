For all the world No 1 s unanswerable supremacy , Murray regarded the prospect of a rematch with Djokovic with calm detachment . The precedent of three consecutive final losses to the Serb in Melbourne held no interest for him . With all the previous disappointments , it s still just one tennis match , he said . It doesn t matter what happened in the past . Murray invoked the example of Stan Wawrinka , who beat Rafael Nadal in the 2014 final despite having never beaten him before . He had beaten Djokovic in the final for both his major glories , he reminded himself , and could do so again .
