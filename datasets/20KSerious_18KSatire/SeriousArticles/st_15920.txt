The DHHS Administration for Children and Families ( ACF ) and Health Resources and Services Administration ( HRSA ) are working with their grantees in the area to disseminate public health education through Head Start , Community Health Centers and other programs to help families understand the risks of lead in the water , encouraging residents to have their children screened , and assisting families in obtaining screening for their children . Medicaid regularly requires that all Medicaid eligible children up to age 6 be tested for lead poisoning treatment for these children is covered under Medicaid . As a diagnostic benefit , home investigations for Medicaid eligible children also are covered under Medicaid . The state can expedite enrollment into Medicaid for eligible children in need of immediate medical attention .
