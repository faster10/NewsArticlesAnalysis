For just as a sworn engagement can bring to so many other couples a sensation of encircling protection , our vow never to marry had cloaked the two of us in all the emotional security we required in order to try once more at love . And this commitment of ours consciously devoid of official commitment felt miraculous in its liberation . It felt as though we had found the Northwest Passage of Perfect Intimacy something that , as Garc a M rquez wrote , " resembled love , but without the problems of love . "
