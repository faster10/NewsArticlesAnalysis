Then there is the fraught politics . The year started badly with Saudi Arabia cutting diplomatic ties with its main rival Iran . Both regional powerhouses are already supporting opposing sides in proxy sectarian wars in Syria and Yemen . Along with the rise of Islamic extremism , these conflicts have turned the region into a tinder box and led to a flood of refugees that is feeding tensions in Europe . In such circumstances , it ' s almost impossible for either Riyadh , or Tehran , to cooperate on oil policy and prevent crude hitting 20 per barrel .
