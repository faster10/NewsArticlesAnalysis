Democratic primary voters are more confident in Clinton than Sanders on a number of specific issues . Fifty three percent of Democratic primary voters are very confident in Clinton ' s ability to handle an international crisis , compared with just 16 percent who are very confident in Sanders ' ability . They are also more likely to be very confident in Clinton ' s ability to make the right decisions about gun laws and the economy . The two candidates are about even on regulating banks and financial institutions , while slightly more voters are very confident in Sanders when it comes to reducing the gap between the rich and poor .
