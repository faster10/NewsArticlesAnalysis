In the wake of the Japanese prime minister ' s visit to Yasukuni shrine last week , Japan ' s struggles to move forward from its wartime past are back in the headlines . Commentators decry that Shinzo Abe ' s visit will increase tensions in East Asia . They are right : this gesture will curdle Japan ' s already sour relations with South Korea or China , and indeed has already provoked the predictable outcry . But Yasukuni visits are a symptom , not the main cause , of Japan ' s poor relations with its neighbors .
