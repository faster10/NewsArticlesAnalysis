With the gaps in the draft agreed between David Cameron and the president of the European council , Donald Tusk , still to be filled and much fudging to be clarified during a fortnight of pre summit Russian roulette at home and abroad , the great debate still feels more like a phoney war . This is a preliminary mobilisation but not yet a cathartic or defining clash between those who think Britain is stronger as part of a true alliance and those who think Britain should stand alone . Those who fear that this country may slip out of Europe in a moment of absentmindedness are entitled to feel nervous .
