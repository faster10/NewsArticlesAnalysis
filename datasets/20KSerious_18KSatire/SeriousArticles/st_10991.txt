The significance of the hotel was as much to do with politics as with hospitality : Conrad Hilton s Jewel of the Nile , built on the site of the British barracks , was the first international hotel to open in the post war Middle East . After the trauma of the 1952 revolution and the overthrow of King Farouk , after the nationalization of the Suez Canal and the 1956 invasion by Britain , France and Israel , here was a sign that Egypt was open for business . Last week I was back in Tahrir to celebrate another opening .
