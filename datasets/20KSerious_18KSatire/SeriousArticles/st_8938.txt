In general elections , Democrats typically rack up votes in urban centers and sweat out the rural returns . But in Iowa s Democratic caucuses , insiders are looking for Clinton to make up some ground in some traditional rural Democratic communities while Bernie Sanders scores big margins in some college towns and cities . Clinton has fanned across the state in the final week of the campaign , while Sanders hasn t held an event west of Des Moines . But both have been battling for votes in areas like Jasper County , a blue collar but traditionally Democratic area just east of Des Moines .
