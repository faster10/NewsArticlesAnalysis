The new proletariats blame digitization as the source of their woes . Content that can be reduced to a series of ones and zeroes loses its value , becoming disposable , they say , so that consumers come to expect content to be free of charge . " Pirating on the internet is rampant , " says a freelance writer working half a dozen day jobs in the service sector , " because that ' s what the internet is designed to do : it turns everything into commonplace information which no one but the author thinks is worth any money . Cultural content now is like an ugly kid with a face only its mother can love . "
