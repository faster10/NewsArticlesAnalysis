The head of the health unit at Sharm el Sheikh airport , Medhat Qandil , said the remains of the last 28 people killed in the crash had been sent to Moscow on Tuesday aboard a military jet , along with luggage from the doomed plane and personal belongings found at the crash site . The bodies of all the other 196 dead passengers and crew had already arrived in Russia , but the final 28 were delayed as the corpses were not intact and required search teams to gather up body parts and conduct DNA tests , Qandil said .
