Turkish television pictures showed the Su 24 exploding and crashing in a ball of flames into a Syrian mountain and two pilots parachuting to the ground after ejecting . A Turkish government official had earlier insisted both pilots were still alive , but Russian military spokesman General Sergei Rudskoi said one had been killed by fire from the ground , citing " preliminary details " . The fate of the second pilot was unknown . Rudskoi said a soldier had been killed in a failed bid to rescue the pair after one of his squadron ' s helicopters was damaged by gunfire and had to land .
