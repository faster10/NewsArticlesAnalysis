Dennis notes that Saudi Arabia almost always spends a lot more than it plans . Still , last year s 14 overspend was smaller than the average overspend of 25 seen in recent years , and Dennis expects the Saudis to keep a tighter rein on spending in 2016 as well given the determination of the authorities to reduce the budget deficit in light of ( a ) diminishing fiscal space , ( b ) the prospect of prolonged low prices , and ( c ) Saudi plans to maintain global market share .
