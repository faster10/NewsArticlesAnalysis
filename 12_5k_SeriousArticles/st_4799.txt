For months , EU and U . K . officials have been discussing the merits of making changes to the EU s basic treaties to accommodate Mr Cameron s demands . U . K . politicians have called for treaty change , but others are very reluctant , not least because it is usually a protracted , politically risky process . Mr Tusk s proposals offered something for both sides . Most of the proposals can be achieved by changing and clarifying EU rules and laws . But the document raises the prospect of embedding at least some of the changes ? rights for noneuro countries and the British exemption from ever closer union ? into future treaty changes .
